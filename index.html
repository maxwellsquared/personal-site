About

Hi! I'm Max, and I'm a programmer and designer. I'm originally from the Bay Area, but I moved to Vancouver to go to UBC where I studied English, art, and film while volunteering for CiTR 101.9FM campus/community radio.

After a decade of working in marketing and communications, I realised that writing code was the part of my work I loved the most, so I made the slightly terrifying decision to quit my awesome job and take a coding bootcamp.

Cthulhu Teaches Typing

A couple of my friends from bootcamp are (like me) passionate about mechanical keyboards but unsatisfied with the websites we found for testing them out. We wanted a simple, clean typing test site that would save your results across time but also keep track of how you perform on different keyboards. Since it didn't exist, we built it. We also threw in plenty of fun extras like competitive multiplayer using websockets, customizable visual representations of your keyboards, graphs of your performance, and sound effects using HowlerJS. There's even a hidden easter egg if you enter the Konami Code.

The backend is Ruby on Rails, while the frontend is a mix of JS, React, and Tailwind CSS.
